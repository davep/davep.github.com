I've just pushed [BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/) v2.3.0 up [to
PyPI](https://pypi.org/project/blogmore/). This release has a [couple of bug
fixes and a couple of significant new
features](https://blogmore.davep.dev/changelog/#v230).

The first new feature, [which came in as a
request](https://github.com/davep/blogmore/issues/332), is to add support
for control over the themes used for code blocks, including independent
control of the themes used for
[light](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#light_mode_code_style) and
[dark](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#dark_mode_code_style) mode.
With these you can specify any of the [Pygments
styles](https://pygments.org/styles/) to use for code blocks. Personally, I
prefer to have things blend in, but this now also gives you the chance to
have them really contrast (use a light mode theme for dark-mode blog, or a
dark mode theme for a light-mode blog).

The other big feature popped into my head earlier today and once I thought
about it I *had* to have it. It's similar to something I had for the
photography section of the [older version of my
website](/2023/08/11/admitting-defeat-on-my-website.html), consisting of [a
bunch of useless but fun stats and facts about the content](/stats/).

Things like which hour of the day I tend to post during:

![Hour of day](/attachments/2026/03/24/hour-of-day.png#centre)

Or the day of the week:

![Day of week](/attachments/2026/03/24/day-of-week.png#centre)

Or the month of the year[^1]:

![Month of year](/attachments/2026/03/24/month-of-year.png#centre)

This is designed to be turned off by default -- I can imagine most folk
would not want this sort of thing on their blog -- [but it can easily be
turned on with
`with_stats`](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#with_stats). The
location of the stats can also be controlled [using
`stats_path`](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#stats_path).

[^1]: [Unsurprisingly](/2026/03/) March is leaping ahead as of the time of
    writing.
