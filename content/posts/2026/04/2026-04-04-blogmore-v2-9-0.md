After releasing [`blogmore.el` v2.6 this
morning](/2026/04/04/blogmore-el-v2-6.html), I noticed something about the
post: the text that was marked up with `<kbd>` wasn't really standing out as
keys. In blog posts, as in documentation, if I mention the name of a key, I
like to mark it up with `<kbd>`. Ideally, with such markup, the styling of
the page it's being used on will make it clear that it's supposed to be read
as a key.

I've never put any such styling into the default styles made available in
[BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/).

So here we are with [BlogMore
v2.9.0](https://blogmore.davep.dev/changelog/#v290), now with a bit of
markup, and theme support, for keys marked up with `<kbd>`. So now,
hopefully, if I say you should press <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>F4</kbd> to make
this blog look better, those keys should stand out a little better than they
used to.
