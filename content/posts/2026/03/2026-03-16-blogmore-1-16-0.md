Prompted in part by the [changes I made
yesterday](/2026/03/15/blogmore-1-15-0.html), I've made another small
release of [BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/) today. The stuff to do
with [human.json](https://codeberg.org/robida/human.json) made mention of
having a `/ai` page, something I remember as being part of the [slash
pages](https://slashpages.net) moment. While I've never leaned into it
myself, I do consider it a fun idea and, given [the current
experiment](/2026/02/20/five-days-with-copilot.html), I did think it made
sense to have a `/ai` page for this blog, at least[^1].

Yesterday's changes, to allow modifying the path used for pages, and also to
apply the [`clean_urls`
setting](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#clean_urls) to them too,
meant it was easily doable now. But I also got to thinking that someone
might not want the page to appear in the sidebar; perhaps they just want the
page there but not obviously linked to.

So with today's release [I've
added](https://github.com/davep/blogmore/issues/290) an [optional `pages:`
configuration setting](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#pages). If
it's omitted, BlogMore works as it always did (listing every page in the
sidebar). If it's included, only the pages in the given list appear in the
sidebar.

Personally I think I'd prefer to have [the `/ai`
page](https://blog.davep.org/ai/) visible in the sidebar, and that's what
I've gone with. But if anyone wants the option of having better control over
this, it's there now.

While I was tinkering on sidebar things I also added a [setting for
overriding the title of the links
list](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#links_title). Again, I think
*Links* works fine for my purposes, for this particular blog, but I can see
the need to want to customise it.

[^1]: I should probably add one to my [main site](https://www.davep.org/)
    too now I think about it.
