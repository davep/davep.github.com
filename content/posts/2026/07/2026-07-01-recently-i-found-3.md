## Introduction

An occasional collection of things I've [recently
found](/series/recently-i-found/) on the Internet and added to [my
bookmarks](/bookmarks/).

## [JSON-LD Explained for Personal Websites](https://hawksley.dev/blog/json-ld-explained-for-personal-websites/)

Handy guide to how JSON-LD can be useful for personal websites.

## [TownSquare](https://townsquare.cauenapier.com/)

On the one hand, this looks like a fun idea: sort of a Second Life for
websites. On the other hand, it's the worst of the Internet, which is no
surprise.

## [The AI Resist List](https://airesistlist.org/)

Does what it says in the title, pretty much.

## [Rest of the World](https://restofworld.org/)

A good reminder that tech stories come from places other than the "western"
world.

## [smolweb](https://smolweb.org/)

A handy guide to all things relating to the small web, and related concepts.

## [Gemtext Is Not Accessible](https://aartaka.me/gmi-a11y.html)

A good reminder that, as fun and neat as Gemtext is, it's not terribly
accessible.

## [Honesty gets Emacs patch rejected](https://xlii.space/eng/honesty-gets-emacs-patch-rejected/)

The FSF, in effect, does what the FSF is there to do: defend the copyright
of GNU Emacs (among other projects). Meanwhile someone attempts to
contribute a patch written by a large language model, and seemingly gets
offended that it's rejected. I think the worst part about it is they seem to
think that being honest about using an LLM should let their contribution in.

## [The UnOfficial History of Second Life!](https://www.reddit.com/r/secondlife/comments/1uj2a2v/the_unofficial_history_of_second_life/)

A pretty fun Reddit post that contains a potted history of the evolution of
Second Life. I've had an account for a good chunk of that time, and it all
reads about right to me.

## [Radial](https://radial.appverge.net/)

I've long been a fan of pie menus. I wish macOS had adopted and used pie
menus. This looks like something I need to try out at some point.

## [The AI Compass](https://bambamramfan.github.io/ai-compass/)

It's one of those "compass" type quizzes that works out where you stand on
the whole issue of AI. I landed on "The Skeptic".
