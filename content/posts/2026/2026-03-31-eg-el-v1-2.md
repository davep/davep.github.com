Okay, that's it then; this is [turning into a thing I
think](/2026/03/31/thinks-el-v1-13.html). Second in an occasional series of
posts where I tidy up some of my old [Emacs packages](https://elisp.dev/).
This time I dug out [`eg.el`](https://github.com/davep/eg.el) and cleaned up
some of the [frowned-upon
behaviour](/2022/08/23/i-must-be-getting-old.html).

For anyone who doesn't know it: `eg.el` is one of *many* [Norton
Guide](/tag/norton-guide/) readers [I've written over the
years](https://www.davep.org/norton-guides/). This particular one was
possibly the most fun as it was the most unlikely one to write and also, I
think it's fair to say, my most ambitious one as well. It also holds a
special place for me in that the bulk of it was written [on
trains](/tag/virgin/) during multiple trips up to Scotland, before I finally
moved here (on the [MacBook Air](/2016/04/28/i-now-own-a-macbook.html) I
[mentioned the other day](/2026/03/27/macbook-air-m5.html)).

As I look at it now, I sort of want to give it a proper revisit. I've
written more Norton Guide code since (see [AgiNG](/2025/03/12/aging.html)
for example) and have learnt better ways to handle certain things, and I
also have an even bigger [Norton Guide
collection](/2025/12/12/norton-guide-collection.html) to test against. All
of this could make for an even better Emacs implementation.

I also suspect that, this time around, I can do a better job of handling the
colour and retaining the original Norton Guide reader look and feel. But,
for now, the code has been tidied up and should keep working for some time
yet.
