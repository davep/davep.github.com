After a day of no movement, I wake up to see this:

![Steam controller is packaged](/attachments/2026/05/06/packaged.webp#centre)

One step closer to getting my hands on it!

I'm oddly kind of invested in getting my hands on this. A regret I have is
that I never bought [one of the
originals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_Controller_(1st_generation)).
Back in the day I did buy (and still own) a [Steam
Link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_Link) and it served me well for a
while, 4 homes ago. The 1st gen controller would have been ideal with that
but somehow I just never got round to grabbing one.

And, sure, while I have Xbox and Sony controllers kicking around (I
especially like my Death Stranding 2 DualSense controller), there's
something very appealing about a controller that feels the way the Steam
Deck does; I find it a really pleasant gaming experience in the hand.

So, this time, I'm not missing out.

My primary intended use at the moment is to pair it with my [Steam
Deck](/tag/steam-deck/) and use it as my "Steam Console", plugged into the
TV while I game from the sofa. Eventually I hope to be pairing it with a
[Steam Machine](https://store.steampowered.com/hardware/steammachine), of
course.

Meanwhile, since moving last year, I've not set up my Windows gaming machine
yet. It's a bit bulky to have in the current living room setup, but not
impossible to set up in the office. Perhaps... perhaps I should actually dig
out the Steam Link again and set it up with that!
