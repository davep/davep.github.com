I've just realised that, somehow, I've managed to post something on this
blog, every single day [this month](/2026/05/).

![May 2026](/attachments/2026/05/31/2026-05.webp#centre)

A large part of this is, of course, because I've been doing a lot of stuff
on [BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/), but there's no getting away from
the fact that BlogMore exists and I feel compelled to make use of it, and
also that [`blogmore.el`](https://github.com/davep/blogmore.el) helps make
it a lot easier to kick off and edit a post.

Thinking back to other blogs I've maintained over the past couple of
decades, I don't think I've ever come close to this. [Last month](/2026/04/)
did come close, broken only by [the couple of days I was chilling in
Whitby](/2026/04/20/whitby.html). [The month before](/2026/03/) also came
close, minus 2 days where I just didn't have something to write.

I don't imagine this will last; in fact, I know for sure that there will be
fewer posts next month (I have a trip coming up). What I do know is that I
feel more compelled to jot something down when an idea turns up, and I'm
enjoying the habit of blogging more frequently. While I expect this run to
calm down, I hope I don't fall back to leaving it months at a time before
opening a fresh Emacs buffer and kicking off some new Markdown.
