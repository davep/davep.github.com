Now that [BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/) is so obviously
feature-complete and there's nothing else I could possibly need to add to
it... here's [v2.32.0](https://blogmore.davep.dev/changelog/#v2320) with a
quick new feature.

The work to add the relatedness of posts got me wondering about other things
that could be measured about the content of posts, and a bit of reading and
looking for ideas resulted in my (re)discovering the [Gunning fog
index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunning_fog_index). That got me
thinking it might be interesting to see what the highest and lowest values
were for my blog, and perhaps what the average is, perhaps even get a feel
for the distribution.

So now BlogMore has a
[`with_gfi`](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#with_gfi)
configuration setting ([and a matching command line
switch](https://blogmore.davep.dev/command_line/#-with-gfi)). When enabled,
it adds the score for a post next to the reading time (if that's enabled, if
not it's in the same location as the reading time would be). It also adds a
load of stats to the [stats page](/stats/).

![The GFI stats for my blog](/attachments/2026/05/28/gfi.webp#centre)

As well as this change, I've also tweaked the stats page so it's easier to
get a link to a particular section. As with headings in posts, when you
hover over a section heading you'll get a little anchor you can click on
that will result in you visiting that particular heading. That should make
it easier to [highlight a particular statistic, if you need
to](/stats/#gunning-fog-index).
