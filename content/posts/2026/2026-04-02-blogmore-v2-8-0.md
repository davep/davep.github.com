I've just published [v2.8.0](https://blogmore.davep.dev/changelog/#v280) of
[BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev) to
[PyPI](https://pypi.org/project/blogmore/). This is a small update which
addresses [a bug that Andy
reported](https://github.com/davep/blogmore/issues/359).

The fix [was simple enough](https://github.com/davep/blogmore/pull/360), and
is another little interesting thing to keep in mind given that BlogMore is
an ongoing [Copilot](/tag/copilot/) experiment. When I first kicked off
BlogMore I let it decide which library to use to handle Markdown (I'm more
used to [markdown-it-py](https://markdown-it-py.readthedocs.io/en/latest/)
via [Textual](https://textual.textualize.io/widgets/markdown/) and so via
[Hike](https://hike.davep.dev/)), and so also let it decide which extensions
made most sense given the request. I've honestly never run into the idea of
[metadata](https://python-markdown.github.io/extensions/meta_data/) before,
only ever dealing with or caring about frontmatter[^1].

On the other hand, I will say this: I was cooking dinner when the report
came in; I pointed Copilot at the issue and let it figure it out. After
eating, clearing things away, and general post-dinner chilling, I dropped
into the repo to see what it had made of it and... it had figured the issue
out and fixed it.

[^1]: I guess technically they're the same thing, but here I mean I'm more
    used to the delimited YAML of frontmatter than whatever it is the `meta`
    plugin was dealing with.
