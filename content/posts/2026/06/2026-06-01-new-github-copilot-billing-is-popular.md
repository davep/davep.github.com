So today is the day, today is when [GitHub Copilot](/tag/copilot/) [swaps to
its new billing system](/2026/05/13/the-copilot-bait-and-switch.html).
Watching [the relevant subreddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/GithubCopilot/)
suggests this might not be popular.

Some folk think it isn't the smartest move.

![Not a good choice](/attachments/2026/06/01/insane.webp#centre)

Some don't feel too friendly towards it any more.

![Friendship ended](/attachments/2026/06/01/friendship-ended.webp#centre)

It looks like some of those friendships have lasted a while.

![2021-2026](/attachments/2026/06/01/goodbye-2021-2026.webp#centre)

Some saw the opportunity to create content out of the situation.

![A cancellation video](/attachments/2026/06/01/cancel-video.webp#centre)

Some have figured out that the thing that costs money, costs money.

![It is too expensive](/attachments/2026/06/01/it-costs-money.webp#centre)

Someone used up half their monthly allowance on just 8 requests.

![Half used after 8 requests](/attachments/2026/06/01/half-after-8-requests.webp#centre)

Although, of course, there's always someone who has to do it better.

![Half after 1 request](/attachments/2026/06/01/half-after-one-request.webp#centre)

To be fair though, at least one person loves the new system.

![Love the new system](/attachments/2026/06/01/love-new-system.webp#centre)

As for my subscription, which came about [after I initially experimented
with free access to the tool](/2026/02/20/five-days-with-copilot.html), I've
not actually cancelled yet, but I can't see me making use of it much more. I
might try a couple of prompts with it, along the lines of what I was doing
while [working on BlogMore](/tag/blogmore/), just to get a feel for how
different the usage is now.

Meanwhile, though, I've found that I'm getting on a lot better with
[Antigravity](/tag/antigravity/) and getting the bits done I want to do. I
suspect this is how I'll keep tinkering with BlogMore, until Google come to
their senses anyway.
