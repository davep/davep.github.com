I've been trying, on and off,
[`copilot.el`](https://github.com/copilot-emacs/copilot.el). I'm kind of
50/50 on it most of the time. Sometimes it seems to really understand the
code around what I'm working on and does a good job of helping complete the
thing I'm about to type; other times it makes wild predictions that just
annoy me and get in the way.

It's interesting to experiment with though.

But, just now, I saw something new[^1]. I'd just started a fresh file and
run [`funhead`](https://github.com/davep/funhead.el). That means that what
had been typed into my buffer was:

```python
##############################################################################
# _
```

and my cursor was sat where you see the `_` above. Then Copilot made a
suggestion:

![Copilot doing its thing](/attachments/2026/06/19/copilot.webp#centre)

Like, sure, we all know that this is what can happen, but damn. Still, of
all the companies involved with or adjacent to AI, I guess they're the ones
to [care the least](https://isaiprofitable.com/).

[^1]: To me, I know others have seen this sort of thing before.
