I've just released [BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/)
[v2.40.0](https://blogmore.davep.dev/changelog/#v2400). This version
contains just one new feature: [post series
support](https://blogmore.davep.dev/writing_a_post/#series).

It's hardly a novel feature, plenty of blog engines support this sort of
thing; I felt it was high time I added it to BlogMore too. The idea is
simple: while [categories](/categories/) provide a method of having
"sub-blogs" within a blog (they also provide their own feeds), and while
[tags](/tags/) let you group together posts that touch on related subjects,
a [series](/series/) will let you stitch together a collection of posts as
directly relating to each other.

Adding a post to a series is as simple as adding a `series` to the
frontmatter of a post.

```yaml
title: Five days with Copilot
category: AI
tags: Python, AI, LLM, Copilot, GitHub, BlogMore, coding
date: 2026-02-20 15:46:00 +0000
series: Agentic Afterthoughts
```

With this done, when a reader is viewing an individual post that is within a
series, some new navigation appears at the top and bottom of the post that
lets them know about the series, and also offers them easy navigation to the
previous and next posts.

![Series navigation](/attachments/2026/06/06/post-navigation.webp#centre)

Also, if the reader were to click on the series title, it will take them to
an archive page for that series, where the posts are presented in the usual
pagination format, but where they're also presented in series order. To be
clear: series order is *always* chronological order.

To help encourage further post discovery, if one or more series are
available, a new `Series` link appears at the top of the blog. This links to
an index of every series available.

![My blog's series index](/attachments/2026/06/06/series-index.webp#centre)

Sorted in alphabetical order, the list also shows how many posts are in each
series and, if [reading time is turned
on](https://blogmore.davep.dev/configuration/#with_read_time), the total
reading time will be shown.

Worth noting is the fact that a post doesn't have to be restricted to just
one series. If you need a post to be in two or more series, you can provide
the series as a list:

```yaml
title: My post that crosses the streams
category: Testing
tags: BlogMore, Series, Test
date: 2022-02-22 22:22:22 +2200
series:
  - Experiments with blogging
  - Eating my own dog food
  - Testing thing
```

The post will then show navigation for every one of those series, and the
post will be in each series' archive page.

Having added this, I've also updated the output of [the `dump`
command](https://blogmore.davep.dev/command_line/#dump-command) so that
`series` (the series list as provided in the frontmatter), `safe_series`
(the series list in their URL-friendly slug forms) and `series_pair` (a list
of series title and slug pairs) properties are available.

As of the time of writing this, I have noticed one issue. The
[linter](https://blogmore.davep.dev/linting/) isn't currently aware of the
URLs that are created as part of the series facility. So if you have a page
or post that [links to the index](/series/), or [links to a particular
series](/series/the-virgin-east-coast-saga/), there will be errors:

```
blogmore lint
Linting site in content...
ERROR: posts/2026/06/2026-06-06-blogmore-v2-40-0.md: Link points to non-existent internal path: /series/ (resolved to /series/)
ERROR: posts/2026/06/2026-06-06-blogmore-v2-40-0.md: Link points to non-existent internal path: /series/ (resolved to /series/)

Linting complete: 377 post(s), 4 page(s), 3 error(s), 0 warning(s) [0.45s].
```

This should be a straightforward fix which I'll make in a follow-up release.
