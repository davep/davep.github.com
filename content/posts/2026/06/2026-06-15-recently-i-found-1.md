## Introduction

An occasional collection of things I've [recently
found](/series/recently-i-found/) on the Internet and added to [my
bookmarks](/bookmarks/).

## [Tutti Space Program](https://tsp.grantkot.com/)

Described as *"A playful 2D space sandbox with flowing materials, heat, and
gravity"*, it's exactly that. One of those things to mess with in the
browser when you're either waiting for a boring meeting, or in a boring
meeting, or just have time to kill. Or just because.

## [The Jqwik Anti-AI Affair](https://blog.johanneslink.net/2026/06/09/the-jqwik-anti-ai-affair/)

The Jqwik author's perspective on their Markdown-based "malware".

## [Finalist](https://www.finalist.works/)

Saw this go past in my Mastodon feed, although I lost track of who mentioned
it. This app falls under the heading of *"apps I really want to like because
it seems to solve a problem I want solved but I don't like how it solves
it"*. Mostly I do like how it solves it, but something is off about it; also
it's laggy as hell on my iPhone 16 Pro, for some reason.

As such, I didn't stick with it for long, but I might revisit it at some
point to see if the performance improves; hence saving it.

## [Modus Themes](https://protesilaos.com/emacs/modus-themes)

In the past few days I've been toying with
[changing](/2026/06/12/simplifying-my-mode-line.html) the
[look](/2026/06/13/it-got-darker.html) of my Emacs. This is where I landed.
The documentation is excellent. I don't know why it took me so long to
discover it.

## [LisaOS Emulator](https://experiments.frontierslab.ai/lisa-emulator/?profile=/lisa-emulator/los31-run.image)

An Apple Lisa. In the browser! I've never used a Lisa. I've never seen one
in person. It's sort of wild to me that I now live at a moment in time where
we can emulate, right in the browser, machines that date from my younger
days.

## [Vintage Macworld Magazine Library](https://vintageapple.org/macworld/)

Never read it myself; I'm a late arrival to the world of the Mac (I've been
a user [just over a decade
now](/2015/06/27/my-first-couple-of-weeks-with-an-imac.html)). Should be fun
reading some of the older copies.

## [Project Gemini](https://geminiprotocol.net/)

This is a new one on me; I just stumbled on it over the weekend. It is a now
well-established protocol for a simple web of documents. I'm *very* tempted
to have a play with this. I'm ever so slightly tempted to see if I can do
something with [BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/), such that I could
target this approach in some way too.
