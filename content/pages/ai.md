## Use of AI

The content of this blog, and its related pages, are all written by and
created by me; none are AI generated. On occasion I will use tools, some of
which will make use of "AI", to check for typos, spelling errors, and so on,
but everything you'll find here is hand-written.

Almost every single project [you'll find on
GitHub](https://github.com/davep/) is hand-coded by me, no AI involved
whatsoever. The stark exception to this is
[BlogMore](https://blogmore.davep.dev/), the static site generator that I
use to generate this blog [from Markdown
sources](https://github.com/davep/davep.github.com). BlogMore is 100%
AI-written: code, documentation, the lot. It was, and remains, [a deliberate
experiment in using AI to develop a whole
project](/2026/02/20/five-days-with-copilot.html).

As time goes on, I may use AI in the creation of other applications. If that
happens the repository and documentation will clearly state this fact.

## Position on use of AI

I am not 100% opposed to using AI tooling when it comes to developing code.
I am concerned about how those tools are created, I am concerned about how
those tools are controlled, I am concerned about the skills and attention to
detail of some of those who employ those tools.

I am, as with so many aspects of the industry I've worked in since 1989,
concerned about how we use new technologies in general.
